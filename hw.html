Pav bhaji, a popular Indian street food, originated in Mumbai during the mid-19th century, created to provide a quick and affordable meal for textile mill workers with limited lunch breaks. The dish consists of a spiced mashed vegetable mixture (bhaji) served with buttered bread rolls (pav).

Vada Pav, often called the "Indian burger," is a beloved Mumbai street food consisting of a spicy potato fritter (vada) nestled inside a soft bread roll (pav), typically served with chutneys. Its popularity stems from its affordability, simple preparation, and bold flavors, making it a favorite among diverse groups in Mumbai and beyond.
